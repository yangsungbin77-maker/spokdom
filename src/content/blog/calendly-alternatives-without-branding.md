Sending a client a booking link that says "Powered by Calendly" looks unpolished, and Calendly only lets you remove it on a paid plan. So if you're hunting for Calendly alternatives without branding, the real question isn't "which tool is cheaper" — it's "which tool actually lets me drop the badge without paying."

This guide answers exactly that. We'll separate the tools that remove branding for free from the ones that just promise customization and then paywall the white-label part. Spoiler: the honest answer is narrower than most listicles admit, and we'll tell you the one option that genuinely delivers a fully unbranded page at no cost.

## Why "Powered by Calendly" is a paid problem

Calendly's free and entry tiers display its branding on your booking page, and removing it is a paid upgrade. You can confirm the current tiers on the official [Calendly pricing page](https://calendly.com/pricing), but the structure is the point: the unbranded, professional look you want for clients sits behind a subscription.

For a freelancer or small business, that badge is a small but real credibility tax. A prospect clicking through to book a call sees someone else's logo on what should feel like your booking experience. It's the kind of detail that quietly signals "free tool," which is exactly the impression you're trying to avoid when you're selling your time.

That's why "without branding" is a category of its own — and why the right alternative depends entirely on whether a tool treats white-labeling as a core feature or a premium add-on.

## What "without branding" actually means

Before the list, it's worth being precise, because tools blur this on purpose. There are three different things people mean by "no branding."

- **No "Powered by" badge** — the vendor's name is gone from your page.
- **Custom colors and logo** — your brand is on the page (often available even when the badge isn't removable).
- **Full white-label** — custom domain, your branding everywhere, no trace of the vendor.

Most "free" customization you'll see is the second kind. The badge removal (first kind) and full white-label (third kind) are what usually cost money — so read each tool's free tier for the badge specifically, not just "customizable booking pages."

## Cal.com — best for fully removing branding free

Cal.com is the honest answer to "unbranded and free." It's open-source (MIT-licensed), and if you self-host it, you get a fully white-labeled scheduling tool with no vendor branding at all, no user limits, and 100+ integrations — for the cost of hosting. Even the hosted free plan is generous, with unlimited event types and bookings.

The catch is the same one we keep hitting: full white-label for free means self-hosting (via Docker or a platform like Vercel), which takes some setup. If you're comfortable with that, nothing else here matches it on price-to-control. If you want the cleanest path to a fully unbranded page and you don't mind a little technical lifting, this is the pick.

## Koalendar — best clean free hosted option

If you want zero setup and a tidy booking page without an intrusive badge, Koalendar is the friendliest hosted choice. Its free plan includes unlimited bookings and scheduling links, and the pages are clean and modern out of the box — far less "free tool" energy than a Calendly free page.

The catch is that the deepest white-label control (custom domain, total branding removal) lives on paid tiers. But for a solo operator who just wants a professional-looking link fast, Koalendar's free tier hits the sweet spot of clean and effortless.

## YouCanBookMe — best for branding control

YouCanBookMe stands out for how much it lets you shape the booking page, with one of the more generous free 1:1 plans around. If your priority is a page that feels like *yours* — colors, messaging, embeddable on your own site — it gives you more design control than Calendly does.

As with the others, the most complete white-label options are paid, but the free tier already lets you put your brand front and center. It's the best fit when "without branding" really means "with my branding instead."

## Zoho Bookings and TidyCal — situational picks

Two more worth knowing. **Zoho Bookings** has a limited free tier and makes the most sense if you already live in the Zoho ecosystem, where it slots in with your CRM and mail. **TidyCal** takes a different approach: instead of a subscription, it's often sold as a low one-time lifetime license, which can be the cheapest long-run way to get a clean booking page if you don't need heavy white-label features.

Neither is the absolute best at free unbranded pages, but each wins in its niche — Zoho for ecosystem fit, TidyCal for paying once and being done.

## How to choose

Match the tool to how much control you need and how much setup you'll tolerate.

- **Want fully unbranded and you'll self-host?** Cal.com, no contest.
- **Want clean and effortless, free, hosted?** Koalendar.
- **Want your brand all over the page?** YouCanBookMe.
- **Already on Zoho?** Zoho Bookings.
- **Prefer paying once?** TidyCal's lifetime deal.

## The honest truth about "free and no branding"

Here's what most listicles won't say plainly: for hosted tools, a completely branding-free page almost always costs money, because the badge is how free tiers advertise. The genuine exception is open-source software like Cal.com, where self-hosting removes both the badge and the bill.

So decide what you're optimizing for. If it's "look professional today with zero effort," accept a clean hosted free tier like Koalendar and upgrade later. If it's "owe nothing to a vendor and remove every trace of branding," self-host Cal.com. Picking the wrong one means either a badge you didn't want or a setup you weren't ready for.
